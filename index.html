The 6-step call flow
01
The Opener
Greet by name. First question must always be open. Mirror their exact words back.
02
Understand Their Situation
Build a picture of what they have today. Keep asking open questions. Let them talk.
03
Find the Gap (1-10)
"Rate what you have today 1-10." "How come it's not a 10?" The gap is everything.
04
Amplify the Need
"What are you missing out on?" "What does that cost the business?" Build urgency.
05
Close for the Meeting
Feed back what you heard. Suggest specific days. You're the expert — guide them.
06
2 Questions Before Hanging Up
Q1: "Who else should I invite?" Q2: "What else should we cover?" Every. Single. Time.
The 1-10 technique
"On a scale of 1 to 10 — where 1 is completely useless and 10 is perfect — how would you rate what you have in place today?"
"How come it's not a 10? What would it need to look like?"
"What are you missing out on by not having that?"
💡 If they say 8 or 9 — "What would a 10 look like?" The number doesn't matter. The gap does. Once they describe the gap and its cost, they've half-sold themselves.
Objection handling quick reference
"I'm in a meeting / bad time" "No problem — when's a better time? I'll lock it in now so I'm not catching you off guard again."
"I'm not interested" "Honestly, you might be right — can I ask you one thing to find out?"
"We already have a solution" "Of course — on a scale of 1 to 10, how confident are you that what you have today will support where the business needs to be in the next 12–18 months?"
"Just send me an email" "Happy to — so I can make it worth opening, what's the one thing most relevant to you right now?"
"We're happy with what we have" "Great to hear. Most people say the same — and when we dig in there's usually one or two things that could be sharper. What would a 10 actually look like for you?"
5 cold call opener styles
STYLE 1 — Direct & Confident
Best for: Senior buyers, ops personas, people who hate small talk
"Hi [Name], it's [You] from Qlik. I'll be straight — we've been working with [industry] companies struggling with [pain], and helped them [outcome]. Is that on your radar?"
STYLE 2 — Curiosity / Pattern Interrupt
Best for: Data personas, anyone who gets a lot of cold calls
"Hi [Name], bit of an unusual call — I'm not sure if what we do is even relevant to you. Can I take 60 seconds to explain, and you tell me if it's worth a conversation?"
STYLE 3 — Insight-Led
Best for: When you have a trigger — growth signal, hiring data, news
"Hi [Name], I was looking at [company] and noticed you've been [expanding/hiring for X]. That usually means [data challenge] becomes a real problem fast. Is that something you're feeling yet?"
STYLE 4 — Referral / Warm Intro
Best for: When you have a name to drop or content touchpoint
"Hi [Name], I think you downloaded / attended [content] recently. I wanted to follow up personally rather than send an automated email — did you get a chance to look at it?"
STYLE 5 — Low Pressure / Disarming
Best for: Hard-to-reach prospects, very senior contacts
"Hi [Name], I promise this isn't going to be a painful sales call. I've got one question — depending on your answer I'll either say it's not worth your time, or ask for ten minutes. Sound fair?"
Gatekeeper golden rules
Short and confident beats long and apologetic every time
Never pitch the gatekeeper — one sentence max on what it's about
Always leave with something: a name, a time, a direct number
A blocked call is not a failed call — it's an intel-gathering exercise
Use the gatekeeper's name if you can find it — it changes everything
Remember
"We are not here to sell. We are here to solve problems. Once someone feels that, they will almost always agree to speak to a specialist. They should feel lucky to get a meeting with us — because we don't just meet with anyone."
